The Client

A B2B Company With a Clean Website and No Digital Presence

The client runs a B2B services business. They had invested in a clean, professional portfolio website — nothing over-engineered, nothing fancy. But it was sitting invisible on the internet.

No one had ever submitted it to Google. No one had checked if it was even crawlable. As far as search engines were concerned, the website didn't exist.

✦ Client Profile

  • ◆ B2B services company — professional, established, credible
  • ◆ Had a clean portfolio site but zero SEO work done
  • ◆ No blog, no keyword stuffing, no meta descriptions
  • ◆ No on-page optimisation of any kind — just clean, well-structured pages
  • ◆ All leads were coming from personal referrals and word of mouth

What We Actually Did

Four Steps. That's Literally It.

The entire engagement was four basic steps that most businesses never bother to complete. No ongoing retainer. No elaborate strategy. Just the foundational work that lets Google do its job.

01

Added Site to Google Search Console

Created a Search Console property for the domain and submitted it. This is the handshake that tells Google "this site is ready to be crawled."

02

Verified Ownership

Confirmed to Google that we're the legitimate owners of the domain — a required step before Google will give you any data or act on your requests.

03

Fixed the Basic Crawl Errors

Search Console flagged a handful of technical errors — broken links, missing pages, redirect issues. We fixed them. Nothing fancy, just housekeeping.

04

Submitted Sitemap & Left It

Submitted the sitemap so Google knew exactly which pages to index. Then we stepped back and let Google work. No further changes to the site.

The Result

A Few Months Later, the Phone Started Ringing

The client sent a message a few months after the setup. Companies were calling them. Emails from businesses who had found them on Google — not from referrals, not from ads, not from LinkedIn outreach. Just organic search.

No paid ads were running. No extra work had been done on the site. The only change was that Google could now find them, crawl them, and show them to people who were searching.

Why It Worked

Google Can't Show What It Doesn't Know Exists

✦ The Simple Reason This Worked

  • ✓ The site already had clean, structured pages — no thin content, no spam, just legitimate business information Google was happy to index
  • ✓ Search Console is the literal door you open for Google — without it, Google crawls your site sporadically if at all; with it, you're inviting it in
  • ✓ Fixing crawl errors removed the blockers — Google couldn't fully crawl the site with those errors in place; removing them let it index everything
  • ✓ B2B buyers search before they call — every prospective client was already Googling for services like theirs; the site just wasn't showing up
  • ✓ Patience is the strategy — organic indexing takes weeks to months, but the leads it generates are free, forever

The key insight: most businesses skip this step entirely. They build a website, share it on social media, maybe run some ads — but never actually tell Google it exists through the proper channel. Search Console is that channel.

The Takeaway

The Simplest Step Opens the Biggest Door

This wasn't a growth hack. It wasn't an SEO campaign. It was just basic digital hygiene — telling Google your business exists and making sure it can read your website properly.

The client didn't need a blog. They didn't need backlinks. They didn't need keyword-stuffed landing pages. They needed Google to find them, and that's a five-minute job if you know where to start.

The lesson for every B2B business with a website: before you invest in ads, content, or campaigns — check if Google can even find you. It probably can't.
